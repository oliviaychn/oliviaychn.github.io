What I Am Passionate About

One of the areas I am most passionate about is the relationship between finance, entrepreneurship, and innovation. I am drawn to environments where ideas are constantly evolving and where people are building things with real ambition. I find it fascinating to study how companies grow, how investors evaluate opportunities, and how markets respond to new information. What makes this interest especially meaningful to me is that it combines both analytical and human elements. Numbers matter, but so do judgment, communication, and long-term vision.

I also enjoy learning technical skills that help me understand the world more deeply. Working with data, building models, and creating structured analyses have taught me how to approach problems with patience and precision. At the same time, I do not see these skills as purely mechanical. I think they are most useful when paired with curiosity. Asking why a trend exists, what assumption drives a result, or how a decision affects people is just as important as producing the result itself.

Beyond academics and professional interests, I value reflection and personal growth. I like writing, documenting thoughts, and keeping track of experiences that shape the way I think. I believe personal interests matter because they influence how we work, what we notice, and what kind of future we want to build. Altogether, these interests motivate me to keep learning, stay open-minded, and continue exploring work that feels both intellectually challenging and personally meaningful.

Some Things I Enjoy

  • Learning about investing, markets, and financial strategy
  • Working with data and building analytical projects
  • Exploring entrepreneurship and startup ecosystems
  • Writing reflections and organizing ideas
  • Designing presentations and visual storytelling
